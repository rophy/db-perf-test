Little's Law check (all three runs fit within 1%):
- iter 13: 3000 threads / 74 ms = 40,540 ≈ 40,661 ✓
- iter 18: 3300 threads / 85 ms = 38,682 ≈ 38,888 ✓
- iter 15: 5000 threads / 149 ms = 33,557 ≈ 33,738 ✓

Avg latency scales superlinearly with thread count, so TPS peaks and declines:
  3000 threads: 40.6K (knee of curve)
  3300 threads: 38.9K (already past knee)
  5000 threads: 33.7K (well past)

CPU 72% at 3300 threads is NOT spare throughput — it is coordinator-wait
idle time, exactly as iter 15 metrics proved (threads block in the
distributed-txn RPC chain, not in CPU).

Verdict:
  3000 threads is the practical TPS optimum for this 6-tserver + trigger
  workload. Any bump past ~3000 trades TPS for queue time. The coordinator's
  per-txn critical path is the cap; no thread count circumvents it.

Next steps:
- Stop tuning thread count.
- Remaining levers: scale out, drop secondary index, or colocate tables.
